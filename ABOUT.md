# Purl

**Save links. Ask questions. Get answers.**

Purl is an AI-powered read-it-later app. Save any link from the web, and Purl extracts, understands, and remembers the content for you. Weeks later, just ask — Purl searches your saved knowledge and gives you answers with sources.

---

## What is Purl?

Purl is a personal knowledge base disguised as a bookmarking tool. The core loop:

1. **Save a link** — from the browser extension, web app, or mobile share sheet
2. **Purl ingests it** — scrapes the full page content, generates a summary, creates embeddings, and auto-tags it
3. **Forget about it** — the link sits in your stash
4. **Ask anything later** — "What was that article about React Server Components performance?" → Purl searches your saved content semantically, finds the relevant links, and gives you an answer citing your sources

The AI chat supports `@mentions` — type `@` to reference a specific saved link and ask targeted questions about it (e.g., `@stripe-docs how does their billing API handle prorations?`).

## How it works

The main interface is a single screen: a minimal list of your saved links grouped by time (Today, Yesterday, Last week), with an AI chat bar pinned at the bottom. That chat bar is the heart of Purl — it's always there, always ready.

**Link cards** — just a favicon, title, and domain. Nothing else. The content lives underneath, ready for the AI to surface when you ask.

**The chat bar** — "Ask anything about your links..." is both the search and the AI interface. Ask a broad question across your whole stash, or `@mention` a specific link to drill into it. Purl responds with answers grounded in your saved content, always citing the source.

## What makes Purl different

Every read-it-later app is a graveyard of links you'll never revisit. Purl solves that by making your saved content queryable. You don't need to remember what you saved, when you saved it, or how you tagged it. You just ask.

- **Pocket** saves and forgets
- **Raindrop** organizes but doesn't understand
- **Notion Web Clipper** clips but you still search manually
- **Purl** understands what you saved and retrieves knowledge on demand
